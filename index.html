Projects
Understanding Performance and Dynamics in Soccer Passing Networks: A Topological-Behavioral Approach
In this project, we create a weighted directional passing network for Everton that quantifies the behavior of players in passes. Spatial and temporal information are also incorporated into the passing network to unveil structural changes on the position and time basis. Then, a comprehensive analysis of the passing network in its topological properties is conducted. Performance indicators are proposed at different topological scales: micro-, meso-, and macro-scale.
In order to study the influence of players’ roles and positions on team performance, the K-means clustering algorithm is used to change players from the original three positions of F, D, and M according to their passing efficiency, shooting efficiency and confrontation efficiency divisions. Finally, the players are successfully divided into 6 categories, and a scheme to adjust the players according to the needs of the formation is proposed.
